\section{Document Structure}
\label{sec:document_structure}
\hspace{\parindent}
This dissertation is organized as follows. Chapter~\ref{chap:ch2} presents the fundamental
concepts required to support the work, including MAPF, graph search, clustering, regression, and
parallel computing. The state of the art in MAPF coordination approaches is then reviewed in
Chapter~\ref{chap:ch3}, which also describes the CRIIS fleet coordination system used as the
basis for this work.

The proposed prioritization heuristics are introduced in Chapter~\ref{chap:ch5}, where
different strategies are developed to generate alternative robot orderings for TEA*. Their individual
behaviour is evaluated in Chapter~\ref{chap:ch6} using an isolated Path Planner
testing framework, allowing the effect of each priority ordering to be analysed independently from
the remaining coordination stack.

Chapter~\ref{chap:ch7} presents the proposed parallel planning framework,
including the worker-thread implementation, heuristic dispatching mechanism, timeout handling,
and redundant heuristic selection strategy. The performance of this framework is then assessed in
Chapter~\ref{chap:ch8}, focusing on the effect of the number of worker threads and
on the heuristic dispatching strategy.

Finally, Chapter~\ref{chap:temporal_consistency_batching} analyses temporal consistency issues
observed during execution of the complete CRIIS coordination stack, focusing on redundant
replanning operations and on the batching mechanisms introduced for planning requests and robot
state updates. Chapter~\ref{chap:ch9} summarizes the main conclusions and outlines
possible directions for future work.
