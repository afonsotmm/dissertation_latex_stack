\chapter{Prioritization Heuristics Design and Development}
\label{chap:ch5}
\hspace{\parindent}
Due to the sequential nature of TEA*, each planned path constrains the following ones, making the
selected robot ordering directly affect solution cost and feasibility. This chapter presents
the prioritization heuristics developed to generate alternative robot orderings for TEA*.

Section~\ref{sec:heuristics_motivation} introduces the motivation and design objectives, while
Section~\ref{sec:heuristic_design_methodology} presents the common score-based formulation.
The remaining sections describe the proposed heuristic families, namely roadmap distance,
topology, surroundings, conflict-based, and replanning heuristics, including their scoring criteria,
ordering variants, algorithms, and computational costs.
