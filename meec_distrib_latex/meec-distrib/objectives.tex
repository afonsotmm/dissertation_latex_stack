\section{Objectives}
\paragraph{}

The main objective of this dissertation is to reduce the sensitivity of sequential centralized MAPF planning to agent prioritization. To this end, the work analyzes the impact of agent ordering on the feasibility and quality of TEA*-based solutions, reviews representative prioritization heuristics from the MAPF literature, and investigates strategies that explore multiple agent orderings instead of relying on a single fixed priority scheme. In particular, it exploits parallel and multi-threaded computation to efficiently evaluate heuristic-driven orderings and experimentally assesses the proposed approach in realistic centralized coordination scenarios, balancing algorithmic improvements with system-level applicability.
